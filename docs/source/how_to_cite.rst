How to cite aPriori
===================

The library's software paper is currently under preparation.

If you use aPriori in your work, please **temporarily cite** the following
peer-reviewed publication, which introduces the methodology and workflows
on which the library is based:

.. code-block:: text

   Piu L, Péquin A, Freitas RSM, Iavarone S, Pitsch H, Parente A.
   A data-driven approach to refine the partially stirred reactor closure
   for turbulent premixed flames. Flow Turbulence Combust. 2025.
   doi:10.1007/s10494-024-00626-3.

Once the dedicated aPriori software article is published, this section will
be updated with the official citation.

Citation formats
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

APA
~~~

.. code-block:: text

   Piu, L., Péquin, A., Freitas, R. S. M., Iavarone, S., Pitsch, H., &
   Parente, A. (2025). A data-driven approach to refine the partially
   stirred reactor closure for turbulent premixed flames.
   *Flow, Turbulence and Combustion*.
   https://doi.org/10.1007/s10494-024-00626-3

Chicago
~~~~~~~

.. code-block:: text

   Lorenzo Piu, Arthur Péquin, Rodolfo S. M. Freitas, Salvatore Iavarone,
   Heinz Pitsch, and Alessandro Parente. 2025.
   “A Data-Driven Approach to Refine the Partially Stirred Reactor Closure
   for Turbulent Premixed Flames.”
   *Flow, Turbulence and Combustion*.
   https://doi.org/10.1007/s10494-024-00626-3

Vancouver
~~~~~~~~~

.. code-block:: text

   Piu L, Péquin A, Freitas RSM, Iavarone S, Pitsch H, Parente A.
   A data-driven approach to refine the partially stirred reactor closure
   for turbulent premixed flames. Flow Turbulence Combust. 2025.

BibTeX
~~~~~~

.. code-block:: bibtex

   @article{Piu2025PaSR,
     author  = {Piu, Lorenzo and Péquin, Arthur and Freitas, Rodolfo S. M.
                and Iavarone, Salvatore and Pitsch, Heinz and Parente, Alessandro},
     title   = {A data-driven approach to refine the partially stirred reactor
                closure for turbulent premixed flames},
     journal = {Flow, Turbulence and Combustion},
     year    = {2025},
     doi     = {10.1007/s10494-024-00626-3}
   }

A Zenodo record exists for software archiving purposes, but the reference
above is preferred for academic citation until the software article becomes
available.
